## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Why Professional Translation Is Required

**AI/Automated Translation Limitations:**
1. **Cultural Nuance** — Cannot capture Canadian French idioms, Latin American Spanish regional differences
2. **Narration Optimization** — Literal translation creates awkward phrasing for spoken delivery
3. **Quality Standards** — CMF grant requires professional-grade bilingual content
4. **Cultural Credibility** — BIPOC storytelling demands cultural fluency, not literal word-for-word conversion

**Example of Translation Complexity:**

**EN (Original):**
> "Toronto, 2015. A woman wakes up speaking Somali. She has been in Canada for twenty years. She speaks English at work. She speaks English to her children. She speaks English in her dreams. Or she thought she did."

**FR (Literal/Bad Translation):**
> "Toronto, 2015. Une femme se réveille parlant somalien. Elle a été au Canada depuis vingt ans. Elle parle anglais au travail. Elle parle anglais à ses enfants. Elle parle anglais dans ses rêves. Ou elle pensait qu'elle l'a fait."

**FR (Professional/Good Translation for Narration):**
> "Toronto, 2015. Une femme se réveille en parlant somali. Vingt ans au Canada. L'anglais au travail. L'anglais avec ses enfants. L'anglais dans ses rêves. Du moins, elle le croyait."

**Differences:**
- Shorter sentences for spoken delivery
- "Somali" not "somalien" (culturally accurate term)
- "Du moins, elle le croyait" more natural than "Ou elle pensait qu'elle l'a fait"
- Rhythm optimized for narration, not reading

## TRANSLATION REQUIREMENTS

### French (FR-CA) — Canadian French

**Translator Requirements:**
- Native FR-CA speaker (Quebec or franco-Canadian background)
- Experience with literary/cultural content
- Familiarity with Black Canadian, Indigenous, BIPOC terminology
- Ability to adapt text for spoken-word delivery

**Cultural Considerations:**
- Use Canadian French vocabulary (not European French)
- Preserve Quebec cultural references
- Maintain Black Canadian historical accuracy
- Example: "porteur de wagons-lits" not "employé de train"

**Narration Optimization:**
- Shorter sentences for breath control
- Natural rhythm for French-speaking narrator
- Pauses indicated by punctuation (not stage directions)
- 2-4 minutes per chapter (same as EN)

**Estimated Word Count:** ~30,000 words  
**Estimated Cost:** $7,500-$12,000 (at $0.25-$0.40/word)  
**Timeline:** 8-10 weeks

---

### Spanish (ES-LA) — Latin American Spanish

**Translator Requirements:**
- Native ES speaker with Latin American background
- Neutral Latin American Spanish (accessible across regions, not Spain Spanish)
- Experience with diaspora narratives
- Ability to adapt text for spoken-word delivery

**Cultural Considerations:**
- Avoid Spain-specific terms (e.g., "vosotros")
- Use neutral vocabulary accessible to Mexican, Colombian, Salvadoran, etc. communities
- Preserve Canadian context (don't make it sound US-centric)
- Example: "mozo de tren" or "maletero" for "porter," not Anglicized terms

**Narration Optimization:**
- Spanish typically 5-10% longer than English
- Adjust sentence length while preserving meaning
- Natural flow for Spanish-speaking narrator
- 2-4 minutes per chapter (may be slightly longer)

**Estimated Word Count:** ~32,000 words  
**Estimated Cost:** $8,000-$12,800 (at $0.25-$0.40/word)  
**Timeline:** 8-10 weeks

## WORKFLOW

### Phase 1: Translator Selection (Week 1-2)

**Where to Find Translators:**
- Canadian Translators, Terminologists and Interpreters Council (CTTIC)
- Literary Translators' Association of Canada (LTAC)
- ProZ.com (filter: literary, cultural, FR-CA/ES-LA)
- Direct outreach to BIPOC francophone/hispanophone communities

**Selection Criteria:**
1. Portfolio review (literary/cultural work samples)
2. Test translation (1 chapter, paid)
3. Community recommendation (if possible)
4. Availability and timeline

### Phase 2: Translation Brief (Week 1)

**Provide to Translators:**
1. All 70 EN chapters (source text)
2. Glossary of key terms (see Season 2 translation framework)
3. Cultural context notes
4. Tone guide: calm, reflective, not academic
5. Narration requirements: 2-4 min per chapter, spoken-word optimized
6. Sample EN audio (if available) for rhythm reference

### Phase 3: Translation Execution (Week 2-10)

**Batch Approach:**
- Week 2-4: Season 2 Stories 1-2 (12 chapters)
- Week 5-7: Season 2 Stories 3-4 (12 chapters)
- Week 8-10: Season 2 Stories 5-6 + Season 3 partial (16 chapters)

**Delivery Format:**
- TypeScript files matching EN structure
- Each chapter: `{ en: "...", fr: "...", es: "..." }`
- Preserve paragraph breaks
- Flag untranslatable terms for review

### Phase 4: Community Review (Week 11-12)

**Cultural Validation:**
- Black francophone reviewers (FR translations)
- Indigenous francophone reviewers (FR translations, Story 2 Season 3)
- BIPOC hispanophone reviewers (ES translations)
- 2-3 reviewers per language

**Review Focus:**
- Cultural accuracy (not grammar perfection)
- Tone appropriate for subject matter
- Pacing works for narration
- No cultural flattening or appropriation

### Phase 5: Revision (Week 13-14)

**Translator Updates:**
- Address community feedback
- Adjust flagged terms
- Final proofreading
- Delivery of completed translations

### Phase 6: Narration Script Adaptation (Week 15-16)

**Narrator Involvement:**
- FR narrator reviews FR translations
- ES narrator reviews ES translations
- Make minor adjustments for spoken delivery (breath marks, pauses)
- Final approval before recording

**Deliverable:**
- 140 narration-ready scripts (70 FR + 70 ES)
- Ready for studio recording
