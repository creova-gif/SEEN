## OBJECTIVE

Establish a professional, ethical narrator casting process that ensures:
- Cultural respect and authenticity
- Audio consistency across platform
- Multilingual quality (EN/FR/ES)
- CMF-grade production standards
- Sustainable talent relationships

---

## CASTING STRUCTURE

### Language Coverage Requirements

**Per Language (EN / FR / ES):**
- **Minimum:** 1 primary narrator (required)
- **Recommended:** 1 alternate narrator (backup/expansion)
- **Total:** 3–6 narrators across all languages

### Assignment Logic

**Narrators are assigned:**
- Per language, NOT per story role
- Consistently across multiple stories where possible
- Based on availability, voice quality, and cultural fit

**Rationale:**
- Maintains audio consistency across platform
- Simplifies production scheduling
- Builds narrator familiarity with content themes
- Reduces cognitive load for listeners (voice recognition = trust)

---

## NARRATOR SELECTION CRITERIA

### Required Qualifications

✅ **Vocal Quality:**
- Neutral, clear accent (not accentless, but accessible)
- Calm, reflective delivery
- Ability to sustain tone across 3–5 minute segments
- Consistent pacing and breath control

✅ **Experience:**
- Long-form narration (audiobooks, documentaries, educational content)
- Comfortable with cultural/historical material
- Professional recording experience (home studio acceptable)

✅ **Cultural Competency:**
- Respectful approach to diverse narratives
- Willingness to receive cultural guidance
- Understanding of diasporic storytelling ethics

### Preferred Qualifications

⭐ **Geographic:**
- Canadian-based talent (CMF preference)
- Familiarity with Canadian cultural context
- Access to professional recording environment

⭐ **Specialized Experience:**
- Diasporic or multicultural storytelling background
- Educational or archival audio work
- Museum/cultural institution narration
- Documentary voiceover

⭐ **Language Proficiency:**
- Native or heritage speaker preferred
- Regional accent flexibility (e.g., Québécois French accepted)
- Ability to handle cultural terminology

### Voice Profile to AVOID

❌ **Incompatible Styles:**
- Influencer/social media voice
- Podcast "host" tone (overly conversational)
- Performative or theatrical delivery
- Commercial/advertising energy
- News anchor formality
- Monotone/robotic delivery

**Why:** SEEN requires intimate, contemplative narration that honors the gravity of cultural stories without melodrama or detachment.

## CASTING PROCESS (STEP-BY-STEP)

### Phase 1: Audition Script Preparation

**Timeline:** 2–3 days  
**Responsible:** Content Director + Audio Producer

**Tasks:**
1. Select 3–4 short excerpts per language (60–90 seconds each)
2. Choose from different story categories:
   - Migration narrative
   - Intergenerational reflection
   - Cultural preservation
   - Identity exploration
3. Ensure excerpts are:
   - Emotionally neutral (not peak moments)
   - Culturally respectful
   - Representative of platform tone
4. Provide pronunciation guides for cultural terms

**Deliverable:**
- Audition script packet (EN/FR/ES)
- Pronunciation guide
- Technical recording specs

---

### Phase 2: Talent Sourcing & Outreach

**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks  
**Responsible:** Production Coordinator

**Channels:**
1. **Canadian Voice Acting Networks:**
   - ACTRA (Alliance of Canadian Cinema, Television and Radio Artists)
   - UDA (Union des Artistes) for French talent
   - Canadian Voice Actors Facebook/LinkedIn groups

2. **Cultural Organizations:**
   - Canadian Multicultural Arts Association
   - Heritage language radio/podcast networks
   - Documentary production companies
   - University language departments

3. **Previous Collaborators:**
   - CBC Radio documentary narrators
   - NFB (National Film Board) voiceover artists
   - Museum audio guide talent

**Outreach Message Template:**
- Project overview (cultural storytelling platform)
- Narrator role description
- Audition requirements
- Compensation range (industry standard rates)
- Timeline expectations

**Compensation Framework:**
- Audition: Unpaid (industry standard) OR $50 honorarium (goodwill)
- Recording sessions: $300–500 per hour (union scale)
- Buyout options for platform rights

---

### Phase 3: Audition Collection

**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks  
**Responsible:** Audio Producer

**Technical Requirements:**
- **Format:** WAV or FLAC (uncompressed)
- **Sample Rate:** 48kHz minimum
- **Bit Depth:** 24-bit
- **Environment:** Quiet, treated space (home studio acceptable)
- **Processing:** Clean, unprocessed (no EQ/compression)

**Submission Requirements:**
- 2–3 audition excerpts (from provided scripts)
- 60–90 seconds per excerpt
- Slate introduction (name, location, language)
- Contact information and availability

**Evaluation Criteria:**
| Criterion | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Clarity | 25% | Pronunciation, enunciation, intelligibility |
| Pacing | 20% | Natural rhythm, not rushed or dragging |
| Emotional Restraint | 20% | Calm without being flat |
| Consistency | 15% | Tone maintained across takes |
| Technical Quality | 10% | Clean recording, low noise floor |
| Cultural Sensitivity | 10% | Respectful interpretation |

---

### Phase 4: Evaluation & Selection

**Timeline:** 3–5 days  
**Responsible:** Selection Committee (Content Director, Audio Producer, Cultural Advisor)

**Process:**
1. **Blind Review (Initial):**
   - Listen to all auditions without seeing names
   - Score independently on criteria above
   - Shortlist top 3–5 per language

2. **Committee Discussion:**
   - Compare notes on shortlisted talent
   - Consider diversity of voice types
   - Assess cultural fit and availability

3. **Final Selection:**
   - Primary narrator per language
   - Alternate narrator (if budget allows)
   - Document rationale for CMF audit

**Decision Criteria:**
- Highest combined score + committee consensus
- Cultural background alignment (preferred, not required)
- Availability for full production schedule
- Budget compatibility

---

### Phase 5: Contracting & Onboarding

**Timeline:** 1 week  
**Responsible:** Production Coordinator + Legal

**Contracting Requirements:**
1. **Work-for-Hire Agreement:**
   - Scope of work (number of stories, estimated hours)
   - Compensation structure (hourly or per-story)
   - Rights assignment (platform usage, streaming)
   - Re-recording provisions (if needed)

2. **Cultural Guidelines Briefing:**
   - Platform mission and values
   - Cultural sensitivity protocols
   - Pronunciation resources
   - Feedback/revision process

3. **Technical Setup:**
   - Recording specifications confirmation
   - File delivery protocols
   - Quality control checkpoints

**Deliverable:**
- Signed contracts
- Narrator contact sheet
- Production calendar

## CASTING RATIONALE SUMMARY

### Selection Philosophy

SEEN by CREOVA narrators are not "performers" but **witnesses**.

Their role is to:
- Carry the story with respect, not dramatize it
- Create space for listener reflection
- Maintain cultural dignity through vocal restraint
- Build trust through consistency and clarity

### Voice Direction Principles

1. **Pace:** Slower than conversational, faster than meditation
2. **Tone:** Intimate without whispering, clear without announcing
3. **Emotion:** Present but restrained, never manipulative
4. **Breath:** Natural pauses, room for listener processing
5. **Energy:** Sustained attention without performance peaks

### Cultural Considerations

- **Accent Diversity:** We value authentic regional accents (e.g., Québécois French, Caribbean-inflected English) over standardization
- **Heritage Speakers:** Preferred for cultural authority, but not required if respect and study are demonstrated
- **Pronunciation Coaching:** Provided for all cultural terms, names, and non-English words

## RIGHTS CONFIRMATION STATUS

### Intellectual Property

All narration recordings are:
- **Work-for-hire** agreements (platform retains rights)
- Licensed for **streaming and on-demand use**
- **Non-exclusive** to narrator (talent may use in portfolio)

### Usage Rights

Platform may use narration for:
- Story delivery on SEEN platform
- Promotional clips (with narrator consent)
- Educational/archival purposes
- CMF reporting and funding applications

Platform may NOT:
- Resell narration to third parties
- Use in commercial advertising without separate agreement
- Alter narration in ways that misrepresent narrator

### Credit Requirements

Narrators will be credited:
- In platform metadata (viewable by users)
- In CMF submission documentation
- In public communications (with narrator approval)

## PRODUCTION WORKFLOW INTEGRATION

### Recording Schedule

Once narrators are cast:

1. **Batch Stories by Theme** (efficient use of narrator time)
2. **Schedule Recording Sessions** (2–4 hour blocks)
3. **Provide Scripts in Advance** (1 week minimum)
4. **Conduct Cultural Briefing** (before first session)
5. **Quality Control Review** (after each session)
6. **Approve/Request Retakes** (within 48 hours)

### Quality Assurance

All recordings must pass:
- Technical QA (noise floor, clipping, artifacts)
- Content QA (pronunciation, pacing, tone)
- Cultural QA (respectful interpretation)

---

## ETHICAL COMMITMENTS

### Fair Compensation

- Industry-standard union rates minimum
- Timely payment (net 30 days)
- Clear scope to prevent scope creep

### Respectful Collaboration

- Narrators receive cultural context, not just scripts
- Feedback is constructive, not punitive
- Right to decline culturally uncomfortable material

### Sustainable Relationships

- Long-term collaboration preferred over one-off gigs
- Professional development opportunities
- Credit and recognition in platform growth

---

## CONTINGENCY PLANNING

### If Primary Narrator Unavailable

1. Contact alternate narrator (if contracted)
2. Re-cast from audition pool
3. Emergency re-audition (expedited process)

### If Quality Issues Arise

1. Provide specific, actionable feedback
2. Schedule retake session (paid)
3. If irresolvable, re-cast with alternate

### If Budget Constraints Occur

1. Prioritize critical stories (onboarding, featured)
2. Delay secondary stories
3. Explore volunteer/community narrators (with ethical considerations)
