## OBJECTIVE

Establish a structured expert review process to ensure:
- **Cultural accuracy** and respectful representation
- **Ethical storytelling** that honors community voices
- **Accessibility compliance** for inclusive design
- **Institutional credibility** for CMF and partnership requirements

---

## CORE PRINCIPLES

### Advisory, Not Censorial

This process is designed to:
✅ Improve quality through expert feedback  
✅ Prevent cultural harm or misrepresentation  
✅ Ensure accessibility and inclusion  
✅ Build institutional trust

NOT to:
❌ Control creative direction  
❌ Override creator ownership  
❌ Delay publishing without justification  
❌ Impose homogenization

### Transparency & Accountability

All reviews are:
- Documented for audit trail
- Attributed to specific reviewers
- Time-bounded to prevent delays
- Subject to appeal/resolution process

## REVIEWER CATEGORIES

### 1. Cultural Advisors

**Role:** Ensure cultural accuracy, respect, and community representation

**Expertise Areas:**
- Community historians
- Cultural practitioners (artists, musicians, tradition-bearers)
- Elders / knowledge keepers (where appropriate and compensated)
- Diaspora community leaders

**Review Focus:**
- Story themes and cultural framing
- Context cards (historical/cultural background)
- Language use (terminology, cultural concepts)
- Representation accuracy

**Example Assignments:**
- Indigenous Languages stories → Indigenous language keeper
- Black Atlantic Canada → Caribbean-Canadian historian
- What We Carry → Trauma-informed cultural advisor

---

### 2. Academic / Institutional Reviewers

**Role:** Verify factual accuracy, historical context, and scholarly integrity

**Expertise Areas:**
- University researchers (migration studies, cultural studies)
- Archivists and librarians
- Museum curators
- Documentary researchers

**Review Focus:**
- Historical accuracy in context cards
- Source citation and attribution
- Institutional partnership alignment
- Academic rigor of cultural claims

**Example Assignments:**
- Voices of Migration → Migration studies professor
- Small Histories → Community archivist
- The First Generation → Diaspora studies researcher

---

### 3. Accessibility Reviewers

**Role:** Ensure platform meets accessibility standards and inclusive design

**Expertise Areas:**
- Caption/transcript quality specialists
- Language clarity experts (plain language, reading level)
- Assistive technology users
- Accessibility consultants (WCAG compliance)

**Review Focus:**
- Transcript accuracy and readability
- Language complexity (Flesch-Kincaid score)
- Cultural term definitions (glossary needs)
- Navigation clarity for screen readers

**Example Assignments:**
- All stories → Accessibility compliance review
- Multilingual content → Translation quality review
- Technical terminology → Plain language review

---

### 4. Language & Translation Reviewers

**Role:** Ensure translation quality and cultural appropriateness across EN/FR/ES

**Expertise Areas:**
- Professional translators (heritage speakers preferred)
- Bilingual/trilingual cultural experts
- Localization specialists

**Review Focus:**
- Translation accuracy (semantic fidelity)
- Cultural adaptation (not just literal translation)
- Tone consistency across languages
- Idiomatic expression appropriateness

**Example Assignments:**
- All trilingual content → Language-specific reviewers
- Cultural idioms → Localization specialist
- Poetry/lyrical language → Literary translator

## REVIEW SCOPE

### What Experts Review

✅ **Content-Level:**
- Story themes and cultural framing
- Historical accuracy and context
- Cultural terminology usage
- Representation and sensitivity

✅ **Language-Level:**
- Translation quality and cultural adaptation
- Reading level and clarity
- Glossary needs for cultural terms
- Pronunciation guides for narrators

✅ **Accessibility-Level:**
- Transcript accuracy
- Caption quality
- Navigation clarity
- Inclusive design compliance

✅ **Metadata-Level:**
- Context cards (historical, cultural)
- Cultural themes tagging
- Content warnings (if applicable)

### What Experts Do NOT Review

❌ **Creative Decisions:**
- Story selection or voice
- Artistic interpretation
- Narrative structure

❌ **Technical Production:**
- Audio quality (unless accessibility issue)
- UI/UX design
- Platform functionality

❌ **Business Decisions:**
- Pricing or monetization
- Partnership agreements
- Marketing strategy

## REVIEW WORKFLOW

### Phase 1: Pre-Review Assignment

**When:** Story reaches "Pre-Publish" status  
**Who:** Content Director

**Tasks:**
1. Identify story category (migration, identity, cultural heritage, etc.)
2. Assign appropriate reviewers based on expertise
3. Notify reviewers with story details and deadline
4. Provide access to story content (text, context cards, translations)

**Timeline:** 1–2 days

---

### Phase 2: Expert Review Period

**When:** After assignment  
**Who:** Assigned reviewers

**Review Window:** 5–10 business days (standard)
- **Urgent:** 2–3 days (emergency only)
- **Complex:** 10–15 days (highly sensitive content)

**Review Process:**
1. Read/listen to complete story
2. Evaluate against review criteria (see criteria below)
3. Document feedback in structured form
4. Submit review within deadline

**Timeline:** 5–10 business days

---

### Phase 3: Feedback Submission

**When:** Within review window  
**Who:** Reviewers

**Feedback Categories:**

**REQUIRED CHANGES** (must address before publish)
- Cultural inaccuracy or misrepresentation
- Accessibility violations (WCAG)
- Translation errors that alter meaning
- Factual errors in context cards

**RECOMMENDED CHANGES** (optional, creator discretion)
- Tone adjustments for cultural sensitivity
- Additional context that would enrich story
- Language clarity improvements
- Alternative phrasing suggestions

**COMMENDATIONS** (positive feedback)
- Effective cultural representation
- Strong accessibility features
- Excellent translation quality

**Timeline:** Immediate upon review completion

---

### Phase 4: Creator Review & Response

**When:** After feedback received  
**Who:** Content Creator + Content Director

**Tasks:**
1. Review all feedback (required and recommended)
2. Implement required changes
3. Consider recommended changes
4. Document decisions (acceptance or rationale for decline)
5. Resubmit for final approval (if required changes made)

**Timeline:** 3–5 business days

---

### Phase 5: Resolution & Publication

**When:** After creator response  
**Who:** Content Director + Reviewer (if re-review needed)

**Tasks:**
1. Verify required changes implemented
2. Approve for publication OR request additional revision
3. Log resolution in audit trail
4. Notify creator of final status

**Timeline:** 1–2 days

## REVIEW CRITERIA BY CATEGORY

### Cultural Accuracy Review

**Criteria:**
- ✅ Cultural concepts represented accurately
- ✅ Terminology used appropriately
- ✅ Community voice respected (not appropriated)
- ✅ Historical context accurate
- ✅ Stereotypes avoided

**Evaluation Scale:**
1. **Excellent:** Deeply respectful, accurate, nuanced
2. **Good:** Accurate with minor clarifications needed
3. **Adequate:** Acceptable but could be improved
4. **Needs Revision:** Inaccuracies or insensitivities present
5. **Unacceptable:** Harmful misrepresentation (publish blocked)

---

### Translation Quality Review

**Criteria:**
- ✅ Semantic accuracy (meaning preserved)
- ✅ Cultural adaptation (idioms localized)
- ✅ Tone consistency across languages
- ✅ No awkward phrasing or "translationese"
- ✅ Cultural terms handled appropriately

**Evaluation Scale:**
1. **Excellent:** Feels native, culturally adapted
2. **Good:** Accurate with minor phrasing improvements
3. **Adequate:** Understandable but stilted
4. **Needs Revision:** Meaning lost or awkward
5. **Unacceptable:** Significant errors, must retranslate

---

### Accessibility Compliance Review

**Criteria:**
- ✅ Transcripts accurate (95%+ word accuracy)
- ✅ Reading level appropriate (Grade 8–10)
- ✅ Cultural terms defined or explained
- ✅ Navigation clear for screen readers
- ✅ WCAG 2.1 AA compliance

**Evaluation Scale:**
1. **Excellent:** Exceeds accessibility standards
2. **Good:** Meets standards with minor improvements
3. **Adequate:** Compliant but could be clearer
4. **Needs Revision:** Accessibility barriers present
5. **Unacceptable:** Major compliance failures

## SCHEDULING LOGIC

### Batching Strategy

**Efficient Review Scheduling:**
- Batch stories by **theme** (all migration stories reviewed together)
- Batch stories by **language** (all French content to French reviewer)
- Stagger deadlines to prevent reviewer overload

**Example Schedule:**

| Week | Stories for Review | Assigned Reviewers | Deadline |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|----------|
| 1 | Migration theme (3 stories) | Cultural Advisor A, Academic B | Feb 17 |
| 2 | Identity theme (2 stories) | Cultural Advisor C, Accessibility D | Feb 24 |
| 3 | French translations (all) | Language Reviewer E | Mar 3 |

---

### Reviewer Capacity Management

**Per Reviewer:**
- Maximum 2–3 stories per week
- Maximum 10 stories per month
- Built-in buffer for complex reviews

**Emergency Escalation:**
- Urgent reviews flagged in advance
- Backup reviewers identified
- Expedited process (2–3 day turnaround)

## ETHICAL PRINCIPLES

### 1. Respect Creator Autonomy

**Principle:** Creators own their stories and have final say.

**Application:**
- Reviews are advisory, not mandatory (except accessibility/factual errors)
- Creators may decline recommended changes with rationale
- Reviewers do not rewrite stories

**Exception:** Stories that risk cultural harm may be escalated to editorial board.

---

### 2. Prioritize Harm Prevention Over Perfection

**Principle:** Reviews prevent harm, not impose perfection.

**Application:**
- Required changes address harm (inaccuracy, insensitivity)
- Recommended changes improve quality (enhancement)
- Reviewers distinguish between "wrong" and "could be better"

---

### 3. Transparency in Feedback

**Principle:** Feedback is clear, specific, and actionable.

**Application:**
- Vague comments ("This feels off") are unacceptable
- Specific locations and suggestions required
- Rationale provided for all required changes

---

### 4. Right to Appeal or Contextualize

**Principle:** Creators may appeal required changes or provide context.

**Application:**
- Appeal process available for disputed feedback
- Editorial board mediates conflicts
- Creator context may override reviewer concern (with documentation)

**Appeal Process:**
1. Creator submits appeal with rationale
2. Editorial board reviews (Content Director + Senior Advisor)
3. Decision made within 3 business days
4. Resolution documented for CMF audit

## CONTINGENCY PLANNING

### If Reviewer Unavailable

1. Contact backup reviewer (maintain list)
2. Extend deadline (if possible)
3. Reassign to alternate expert

### If Feedback Conflicts

1. Convene editorial board
2. Seek additional expert opinion
3. Creator makes final decision with documented rationale

### If Deadline Missed

1. Assess criticality (can story publish without review?)
2. Expedite with backup reviewer
3. Delay publication if necessary (cultural safety priority)
