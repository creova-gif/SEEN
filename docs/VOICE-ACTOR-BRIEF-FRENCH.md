## PROJECT OVERVIEW

**SEEN** is an interactive cultural media platform showcasing Black Canadian, Indigenous, and diaspora stories through audio narration, film, and music. The platform is officially trilingual (EN/FR/ES) and funded by the Canada Media Fund (CMF).

Your voice will bring these stories to life for francophone audiences across Canada and internationally.

## VOICE PROFILE REQUIREMENTS

### Vocal Qualities

**Accent:**
- **Canadian French (FR-CA)** — Quebec or franco-Canadian accent preferred
- Not European French (France accent)
- Neutral enough to be accessible across francophone Canada

**Tone:**
- Calm, reflective, steady
- Conversational, not theatrical
- Warm but not sentimental
- Matter-of-fact but not cold

**Delivery Style:**
- Spoken-word narration (not character performance)
- Emotion conveyed through rhythm and pacing, not vocal emphasis
- Natural pauses between paragraphs
- Clear articulation for accessibility
- No dramatic inflection or "radio voice"

**Reference Tone:**
- NPR/Radio-Canada documentary narration
- Ira Glass ("This American Life") — calm, conversational
- NOT audiobook character performance
- NOT news anchor delivery

### Gender & Age

**Gender:** No preference (any gender identity welcome)  
**Age Range:** 30-55 years old (mature, grounded voice)  
**Multiple Voices:** We may use 2 narrators (one for reflective stories, one for urgent stories)

## RECORDING REQUIREMENTS

### Technical Specifications

**Format:**
- WAV (uncompressed audio)
- 48kHz sample rate (broadcast standard)
- 24-bit depth
- Mono (single channel)

**Delivery Format:**
- MP3 (320kbps, compressed for web)
- Mono
- Normalized to -16 LUFS (podcast standard)

**Quality:**
- Professional studio recording
- No background noise
- No mouth clicks or excessive breaths
- Clean, broadcast-quality audio

### File Naming Convention

```
Season{X}_Story{Y}_Chapter{Z}_FR.wav
```

**Examples:**
- `Season2_Story1_Chapter1_FR.wav`
- `Season2_Story1_Chapter2_FR.wav`
- `Season3_Story1_Chapter1_FR.wav`

### Recording Environment

**Studio Requirements:**
- Soundproof/acoustically treated space
- Professional condenser microphone (Neumann U87 or equivalent)
- Audio interface with 48kHz/24-bit capability
- Pop filter
- Audio engineer (optional but preferred)

**Home Studio Alternative:**
- If recording at home, must submit test recording for quality approval
- Must demonstrate professional-level audio quality
- Background noise must be eliminated

## RECORDING PROCESS

### Pre-Production

**Step 1: Script Review**
- Receive all 70 FR narration scripts
- Review for pronunciation questions
- Flag any terms needing clarification
- Estimated review time: 3-4 hours

**Step 2: Test Recording**
- Record 1 chapter (paid)
- Submit for approval
- Adjust delivery based on feedback
- Final approval before full recording

### Production

**Recording Sessions:**
- **Session 1 (4 hours):** Season 2 Stories 1-2 (12 chapters)
- **Session 2 (4 hours):** Season 2 Stories 3-4 (12 chapters)
- **Session 3 (4 hours):** Season 2 Stories 5-6 + Season 3 Stories 1-2 (18 chapters)
- **Session 4 (4 hours):** Season 3 Stories 3-6 (28 chapters)

**Total Studio Time:** 16 hours (spread across 2-4 weeks)

**Recording Workflow:**
- Engineer provides script on screen or in hand
- Read each chapter 1-2 times (variations for editing)
- Flag any stumbles or re-dos
- Breaks every 45-60 minutes

### Post-Production (Provided by CREOVA)

- Remove breaths, clicks, background noise
- Normalize audio to -16 LUFS
- Add 1-second fade in/out
- Export as WAV + MP3
- Deliver final files to voice actor for approval

## DIRECTION NOTES

### For Reflective Stories (Season 2 Stories 1, 3, 5 / Season 3 Stories 1, 2, 4, 5)

**Pacing:**
- Slower, contemplative
- Natural pauses between paragraphs (2-3 seconds)
- Allow silence to carry weight

**Emphasis:**
- Through pacing, not volume
- Let the text speak for itself
- Trust the listener's intelligence

**Example Feel:**
- Someone sharing a memory at a kitchen table
- Thoughtful, present, unhurried

### For Urgent Stories (Season 2 Stories 2, 4, 6 / Season 3 Stories 3, 6)

**Pacing:**
- Tighter, more direct
- Shorter pauses (1-2 seconds)
- Forward momentum

**Emphasis:**
- Through precision and clarity
- Confidence without aggression
- Investigative journalism tone

**Example Feel:**
- Someone explaining something important
- Clear, focused, purposeful

### General Direction

**DO:**
- Read as if speaking to one person, not a crowd
- Use natural vocal inflection
- Breathe naturally (we'll edit out excessive breaths)
- Take breaks when needed
- Ask questions if unsure

**DON'T:**
- Perform characters or dialogue
- Add dramatic pauses not indicated by punctuation
- Over-emphasize emotional moments
- Rush through difficult sections
- Try to sound "professional" (be natural)

### Sample Text for Audition (Season 3, Story 1, Chapter 1 — excerpt)

```
Toronto, 2015. Une femme se réveille en parlant somali.

Elle vit au Canada depuis vingt ans. Elle parle anglais au travail. 
Elle parle anglais à ses enfants. Elle parle anglais dans ses rêves. 
Du moins, elle le croyait.

Mais ce matin, elle se réveille et les premiers mots qui sortent de 
sa bouche sont en somali. Des mots qu'elle n'a pas prononcés depuis 
des mois. Des mots que ses enfants ne comprennent pas.

Sa fille la regarde, confuse. « Maman, qu'est-ce que tu dis? »

Et elle réalise qu'elle ne sait pas comment le traduire. Non pas 
parce que les mots sont difficiles. Mais parce que le sens ne se 
transfère pas. Le sentiment derrière les mots n'existe qu'en somali.

Voici la diaspora. Voici vivre entre deux langues. Voici se réveiller 
dans un pays et rêver dans un autre.
```

**Direction for Audition:**
- Read naturally, as if telling a story to one person
- Calm, reflective tone
- Natural pauses between paragraphs
- Do not over-dramatize
