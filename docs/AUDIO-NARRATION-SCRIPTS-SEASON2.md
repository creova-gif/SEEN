## STORY 1: THE BLACK CANADIAN RENAISSANCE

**Total Recording Time:** 25 minutes  
**Narrator Profile:** Warm, reflective  
**Chapters:** 6

### Chapter 1: The Quiet Revolution (4 min)

**Narrator Tone:** Reflective, calm  
**Pacing:** Moderate, allow pauses

```
Montreal, 1968.

[Pause]

The city is changing. Students march. Workers strike. Quebec demands recognition.

[Pause]

And in the basement of a church on Saint-Laurent Boulevard, a small group of Black writers gather around a borrowed typewriter.

They call themselves the Negro Community Centre Writers' Workshop. The name will not age well, but the work will endure.

[Pause]

Austin Clarke sits at the head of the table. He has just published his first novel. Beside him, a young journalist named Donna Williams takes notes. Across from them, a poet from Trinidad experiments with Creole rhythms in English verse.

This is not the Harlem Renaissance. This is not the Black Arts Movement. This is something else.

[Pause]

Something quieter. Something Canadian.

[Continue with full chapter text, maintaining natural pauses...]
```

**Recording Notes:**
- Pronounce "Creole" as KREE-ole
- Pause after "Montreal, 1968" for atmosphere
- Emphasize "Something quieter. Something Canadian." as thesis statement
- Final line "This is the beginning" should be quiet, certain

### Chapter 2: The Publishing Wars (4 min)

**Narrator Tone:** Urgent, direct  
**Pacing:** Faster, sharper

```
Toronto, 1975.

[Pause]

Dionne Brand walks into a publisher's office with a manuscript. She is twenty-two years old. The poems are about Trinidad, about migration, about being a Black woman in a white country.

The editor flips through the pages. He looks up.

"This is very… ethnic," he says. "Do you have anything more universal?"

[Brief pause]

Brand takes her manuscript and leaves. She will not try again with white publishers. Not yet.

[Continue with full chapter text...]
```

**Recording Notes:**
- "Ethnic" should sound dismissive (as editor would say it)
- Emphasize "She will not try again" with quiet defiance
- Pick up pace during Williams-Wallace Publishers section
- Final line "You build your own house" should be strong, declarative

## STORY 2: THE SLEEPING CAR PORTERS

**Total Recording Time:** 28 minutes  
**Narrator Profile:** Urgent, direct  
**Chapters:** 6

### Chapter 1: The Job Nobody Wanted (5 min)

**Narrator Tone:** Somber, steady  
**Pacing:** Slow, deliberate

```
Winnipeg, 1919.

[Long pause]

A white manager at Canadian Pacific Railway stands in front of a room full of Black men. He does not look at them directly. He speaks to the wall behind them.

"The job is simple," he says. "You make the beds. You shine the shoes. You serve the meals. You keep the passengers happy. You do not complain. You do not sit. You do not sleep. You smile."

[Pause]

The men listen. They know this is the only job available to them.

[Continue with full chapter text...]
```

**Recording Notes:**
- Manager's voice should be cold, detached
- List of job duties ("make the beds," "shine the shoes") rapid, mechanical
- Slow down for "But they learn" section
- Final line "This is a story about refusal" should be clear, firm

---

### Chapter 4: The Women Who Waited (5 min)

**Narrator Tone:** Reflective, honoring  
**Pacing:** Moderate, allow silence

```
They do not appear in the photographs. They do not appear in the union records.

[Pause]

But they were there.

[Long pause]

The wives of the porters. The mothers. The daughters. The women who held families together while the men were on the rails.

[Continue with full chapter text...]
```

**Recording Notes:**
- "But they were there" should be quiet but powerful
- Woman's quote "My husband built this railway" needs dignity, strength
- "This is that credit. This is that acknowledgment." — emphasize each word
- Allow longer pauses throughout this chapter (honoring silence)

## RECORDING SPECIFICATIONS

### Technical Requirements

**Audio Format:**
- Recording: WAV (48kHz, 24-bit, stereo)
- Delivery: MP3 (320kbps) + WAV master
- Noise floor: <-60dB
- Room tone: 5 seconds at beginning and end

## POST-PRODUCTION NOTES

### Minimal Editing Required

These scripts are designed for **minimal post-production**:
- ✅ Natural breathing is part of the performance (do not remove)
- ✅ Pauses are intentional (do not shorten)
- ✅ Slight imperfections are human (do not over-process)

### Processing Chain (If Needed)

1. **Noise Reduction:** Very light (only if needed)
2. **EQ:** Slight warmth boost (100-300Hz), clarity boost (3-5kHz)
3. **Compression:** Gentle (3:1 ratio, slow attack/release)
4. **Normalization:** -3dB peak
5. **Export:** MP3 320kbps + WAV master

### Quality Standards

**Good:**
- ✅ Clear, intelligible speech
- ✅ Natural breathing and pauses
- ✅ Slight room tone (adds warmth)
- ✅ Consistent volume

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Over-processed (too clean, artificial)
- ❌ No breathing (sounds inhuman)
- ❌ Too loud (clipping, distortion)
- ❌ Flat affect (monotone delivery)
