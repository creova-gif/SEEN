## PHASE 6: AMBIENT AUDIO PRODUCTION ✅

### Sound Design Categories (5)

**1. Urban Night**
- **Brief:** Late-night city soundscapes, street ambience, distant traffic, occasional footsteps
- **Emotional Intent:** Contemplative, solitary, urban memory
- **Loop Length:** 3-5 minutes
- **Mixing Level:** -15dB to -18dB relative to narration
- **Usage:** Urban migration stories, night work narratives, city life

**2. Interior Memory Spaces**
- **Brief:** Room tone, creaking floors, distant voices, ambient household sounds
- **Emotional Intent:** Intimate, nostalgic, familial
- **Loop Length:** 2-4 minutes
- **Mixing Level:** -18dB to -20dB (very subtle)
- **Usage:** Family stories, home narratives, domestic life

**3. Nature / Land**
- **Brief:** Water (rivers, ocean), wind, birds, rustling leaves, natural environments
- **Emotional Intent:** Connection to land, Indigenous land-based knowledge
- **Loop Length:** 4-5 minutes
- **Mixing Level:** -12dB to -15dB
- **Usage:** Indigenous stories, migration journeys, land connection narratives

**4. Transit & Movement**
- **Brief:** Trains, buses, footsteps, travel sounds, departure/arrival ambience
- **Emotional Intent:** Journey, displacement, movement between worlds
- **Loop Length:** 3-4 minutes
- **Mixing Level:** -15dB to -18dB
- **Usage:** Migration stories, Underground Railroad, displacement narratives

**5. Abstract Cultural Texture**
- **Brief:** Experimental soundscapes, cultural sonic fragments, memory textures
- **Emotional Intent:** Disorientation, memory, cultural layering
- **Loop Length:** 2-5 minutes (can be non-looping)
- **Mixing Level:** -15dB to -20dB (varies by story)
- **Usage:** Abstract narratives, memory pieces, cultural identity stories

### Integration Metadata

**Technical Specifications:**
- **Sample Rate:** 48kHz
- **Bit Depth:** 24-bit
- **Format:** WAV (master), MP3 320kbps (delivery)
- **Stereo:** Yes (binaural preferred for intimate spaces)
- **Normalization:** -3dB peak

**Story World Assignments:**
- Urban Night → Midnight Resonance, Caribbean Migration, Urban labor stories
- Interior Memory → Family stories, What We Carry, Letters Never Sent
- Nature / Land → Indigenous stories, Black Loyalists (ocean), Land Back
- Transit & Movement → Underground Railroad, Migration stories, Refugees
- Abstract Texture → Memory pieces, Identity narratives, Cultural hybridity

### Accessibility Notes

All ambient soundscapes must have audio descriptions for deaf/HoH users:
- "Distant city traffic, occasional footsteps, urban night ambience"
- "Gentle ocean waves, seabirds calling, coastal wind"
- "Train rhythmic movement, distant conversations, travel sounds"

## PHASE 8: MODERATOR TRAINING ✅

### Training Categories (4)

**1. Cultural Sensitivity**

**Definition:** Understanding cultural context, avoiding harm, respecting community voices

**Review Examples:**
- ✅ APPROVE: Content that centers BIPOC voices, challenges systemic racism, documents community resistance
- ❌ REMOVE: Content that stereotypes, appropriates, or erases BIPOC experiences
- ⚠️ ESCALATE: Content requiring cultural advisor consultation (religious practices, sacred knowledge, community-specific protocols)

**Decision Criteria:**
- Does content respect community self-representation?
- Are cultural practices described accurately and respectfully?
- Is there potential for harm to specific communities?
- Has community consultation occurred where appropriate?

**Escalation Rules:**
- Indigenous content → Consult Indigenous Advisory Council
- Black Canadian content → Consult Black history advisors
- LGBTQ2S+ content → Consult LGBTQ2S+ community advisors
- Religious/spiritual content → Consult relevant community leaders

**2. Harm Prevention**

**Definition:** Identifying content that causes harm (hate speech, violence, trauma triggers)

**Review Examples:**
- ✅ APPROVE: Historical documentation of violence with content warnings, educational context
- ❌ REMOVE: Hate speech, slurs without reclamation context, gratuitous violence, harassment
- ⚠️ ESCALATE: Ambiguous cases, potential for re-traumatization, threats

**Decision Criteria:**
- Does content violate SEEN content policy?
- Is violence/trauma documented for educational purposes with appropriate warnings?
- Could content cause immediate harm to users or communities?
- Is there context that mitigates potential harm?

**Escalation Rules:**
- Immediate threats → Remove + admin review + law enforcement if necessary
- Hate speech → Remove + user warning/ban
- Graphic content → Content warning required or escalate
- Trauma triggers → Add trigger warnings or escalate for review

**3. Accessibility & Language**

**Definition:** Ensuring content is accessible to all users (transcripts, captions, translations)

**Review Examples:**
- ✅ APPROVE: Content with full transcripts, captions, audio descriptions, multilingual options
- ❌ FLAG: Missing transcripts, untranslated content, inaccessible formatting
- ⚠️ ESCALATE: Technical accessibility barriers, screen reader incompatibility

**Decision Criteria:**
- Does content meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards?
- Are transcripts and captions provided?
- Is content available in required languages (EN/FR minimum)?
- Are audio descriptions provided for visual content?

**Escalation Rules:**
- Missing transcripts → Flag for accessibility team
- Untranslated required content → Flag for translation
- Technical barriers → Escalate to development team
- User-reported accessibility issues → Immediate review

**4. Community Care & Restoration**

**Definition:** Supporting users, repairing harm, restorative justice approaches

**Review Examples:**
- ✅ APPROVE: User feedback addressed with care, harm acknowledged, restoration offered
- ❌ REJECT: Dismissing user concerns, defensive responses, no accountability
- ⚠️ ESCALATE: Complex harm situations, community conflicts, systemic issues

**Decision Criteria:**
- Has affected user/community been centered in resolution?
- Is there accountability for harm caused?
- Are restorative measures appropriate and sufficient?
- Does resolution align with SEEN values?

**Escalation Rules:**
- Individual harm → Direct user support + content review
- Community harm → Involve cultural advisors + community leaders
- Systemic issues → Escalate to platform governance team
- Ongoing conflicts → Mediation + restorative justice process

### Opt-Out Enforcement Logic

**User Consent Flow:**
```
1. First App Launch
   → Show consent banner
   → Explain data collection (aggregate only)
   → Offer opt-out
   → Log consent decision

2. If Opt-Out Selected
   → Set analytics_disabled = true
   → No data collection begins
   → Confirmation message shown

3. Periodic Consent Review
   → Annual reminder
   → Option to change consent
   → Clear explanation of what's tracked

4. Settings Access
   → Analytics toggle in Privacy Settings
   → Immediate effect on opt-out
   → No retroactive data deletion needed (because aggregate only)
```
