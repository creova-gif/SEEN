## SEASON 2: DEEPER ROOTS
**Target Release:** Fall 2026  
**Theme:** Land, language, and Indigenous sovereignty  
**Story Count:** 12 Story Worlds

---

### S2.1: Language is Land
**Working Title:**
- EN: Language is Land
- FR: La Langue est Terre
- ES: El Idioma es Tierra

**Theme:** Indigenous language revitalization and territorial connection

**Format:** Story (Audio narration + context cards)

**Estimated Chapters:** 6

**Target Audience:** Indigenous communities, language learners, settlers learning Treaty relationships

**Cultural Context:**  
Explores the connection between Indigenous languages and land-based knowledge. Features Anishinaabemowin, Inuktitut, and Mi'kmaq perspectives on language as more than communication — as relationship to territory, ancestors, and future generations.

**Institutional Partners:** Indigenous language nests, First Nations University of Canada

**Release Season:** Fall 2026

---

### S2.2: Two-Spirit Futures
**Working Title:**
- EN: Two-Spirit Futures
- FR: Avenirs Bispirituel
- ES: Futuros Dos Espíritus

**Theme:** Indigenous gender diversity and LGBTQ2S+ identity

**Format:** Story (Audio narration + visual elements)

**Estimated Chapters:** 5

**Target Audience:** Two-Spirit community, LGBTQ2S+ youth, Indigenous youth

**Cultural Context:**  
Two-Spirit identity as Indigenous-specific, not Western LGBTQ+ import. Stories of reclaiming pre-colonial gender roles, navigating colonial binary systems, and building community across urban and reserve contexts.

**Institutional Partners:** 2-Spirits in Motion, Indigenous LGBTQ2S+ organizations

**Release Season:** Fall 2026

---

### S2.3: Water Keepers
**Working Title:**
- EN: Water Keepers
- FR: Gardiens de l'Eau
- ES: Guardianes del Agua

**Theme:** Indigenous water protection and environmental sovereignty

**Format:** Story + Short Film

**Estimated Chapters:** 4 stories + 1 documentary short

**Target Audience:** Environmental activists, Indigenous youth, water protectors

**Cultural Context:**  
Water as sacred, as relative, as responsibility. Stories from Standing Rock, Grassy Narrows mercury poisoning, Elsipogtog anti-fracking resistance, and Wet'suwet'en land defense. Not romanticized — real stories of sacrifice, resistance, and ongoing threats.

**Institutional Partners:** Indigenous Climate Action, water protection networks

**Release Season:** Fall 2026

---

### S2.4: Urban Indigeneity
**Working Title:**
- EN: Urban Indigeneity
- FR: Indigénéité Urbaine
- ES: Indigeneidad Urbana

**Theme:** Indigenous identity in Canadian cities

**Format:** Story (Audio narration)

**Estimated Chapters:** 6

**Target Audience:** Urban Indigenous people, settlers in cities

**Cultural Context:**  
Majority of Indigenous people in Canada live in cities. Stories of maintaining identity, ceremony, and community away from reserves. Friendship centres, urban pow wows, navigating racism, and building chosen family.

**Institutional Partners:** Urban Indigenous organizations, Friendship Centres

### S2.7: Inuit Nunangat
**Working Title:**
- EN: Inuit Nunangat
- FR: Inuit Nunangat
- ES: Inuit Nunangat

**Theme:** Inuit homeland and contemporary life

**Format:** Story + Audio Album (throat singing, traditional music)

**Estimated Chapters:** 4 stories + 1 audio collection

**Target Audience:** Inuit communities, southern Canadians

**Cultural Context:**  
Nunangat as homeland — not "the North" from southern perspective. Climate change, food sovereignty, housing crisis, suicide epidemic, and also: thriving culture, strong communities, incredible resilience.

**Release Season:** Fall 2026

### S2.8: Residential School Legacies
**Working Title:**
- EN: What They Took / What Remains
- FR: Ce Qu'ils Ont Pris / Ce Qui Reste
- ES: Lo Que Tomaron / Lo Que Queda

**Theme:** Intergenerational trauma and healing from residential schools

**Format:** Story (Audio narration with survivor testimony)

**Estimated Chapters:** 6

**Target Audience:** Survivors, descendants, educators, general public

**Cultural Context:**  
Truth without reconciliation performance. Real stories from survivors and descendants. Trauma, yes. But also resistance, survival, and refusal to let that define all of Indigenous life. Content warnings throughout.

**Institutional Partners:** National Centre for Truth and Reconciliation, survivor networks

**Release Season:** Fall 2026 (Sept 30 — National Day for Truth and Reconciliation)

### S2.11: Sixties Scoop Survivors
**Working Title:**
- EN: Stolen Childhoods

- ES: Infancias Robadas

**Theme:** Sixties Scoop and child welfare system

**Format:** Story (Audio narration)

**Estimated Chapters:** 5
