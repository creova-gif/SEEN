## Q&A PREPARATION

### Anticipated Questions

**Q: How is this different from Netflix or Spotify?**

A: "SEEN is not a streaming service. We're an archive. We don't optimize for 
engagement or retention. We optimize for cultural impact and educational value. 

No algorithms. No autoplay. No infinite scroll. Users choose what they engage 
with, not what an algorithm pushes on them.

And we're privacy-first. No surveillance. No behavioral tracking. No data selling."

---

**Q: How do you ensure content quality without algorithms?**

A: "Human curation. Every film, every story, every editorial section is selected 
by our team based on cultural relevance, educational value, and community feedback.

We partner with universities, museums, and cultural institutions to ensure 
academic rigor. Content is reviewed for accuracy, cultural sensitivity, and 
Canadian specificity.

Quality over quantity. Curation over automation."

---

**Q: Is all content free?**

A: "SEEN is designed as a public cultural resource. Our model is grant-funded 
and institutional partnerships, not user subscriptions or ads.

CMF support allows us to keep content freely accessible while compensating 
creators fairly. We're exploring institutional licensing for universities and 
museums as a sustainable revenue model.

But the core platform remains free and accessible to the public."

---

**Q: What about copyright and film licensing?**

A: "All 20 films are from legitimate sources—NFB, CBC, Historica Canada, 
educational institutions. We've verified embedding permissions.

For original story worlds, we own the content. Narrators are contracted with 
platform-wide usage rights. Music is either licensed or commissioned with 
proper rights clearance.

We're rights-safe. Everything is documented."

---

**Q: How do you measure success without engagement metrics?**

A: "We measure cultural impact, not engagement.

Metrics we track (aggregate only):
- Which stories reach underserved communities
- Which collections are used in university courses
- Geographic reach (are we reaching all provinces?)
- Language distribution (EN/FR/ES usage)

We don't track:
- Time on platform (engagement optimization)
- Click-through rates (algorithmic training)
- Individual user behavior (surveillance)

Success = cultural reach and educational impact, not screen time."

---

**Q: What's your content moderation strategy?**

A: "Three-tier moderation system:

1. Community Moderators — Peer-led, cultural gatekeeping
2. Institutional Moderators — University/museum liaisons
3. Platform Moderators — CREOVA staff, policy enforcement

Training curriculum developed. Escalation protocols in place.

But importantly: SEEN is not user-generated content. It's curated. 
So moderation needs are minimal—no comment sections, no user uploads, 
no social features.

This is intentional. We're not building a social platform. We're building 
a cultural archive."

---

**Q: Is this just for Black Canadians?**

A: "SEEN centers BIPOC stories—Black, Indigenous, and people of color. 
That's intentional.

But the platform is for all Canadians. These are Canadian stories. 
They belong in the national narrative, not as 'diverse' add-ons but 
as core history.

We're not segregating content. We're centering voices that have been 
marginalized. That's different."

---

**Q: What happens if CMF funding isn't approved?**

A: "We'll continue building with alternate funding—private donors, 
foundation grants, institutional partnerships.

But CMF support accelerates our timeline significantly. It allows us 
to commission original content, hire narrators, and launch publicly 
in 2026 instead of 2027-2028.

CMF funding makes the difference between a small, slow rollout and 
a full public launch that reaches millions of Canadians."
